About Me

I am a Food Scientist with a degree from the Federal University of Mato Grosso (Brazil) and a Ph.D. in Food Science from the Federal University of Rio de Janeiro (Brazil), with expertise in Food Microbiology and Bioinformatics.
Currently, I am starting a new research position at the University of Lethbridge (Canada), funded by Mitacs and BCRC.
My objective is to understand and develop strategies to prevent biofilm formation by Escherichia coli in slaughterhouses.

I am passionate about open science, reproducibility, and collaborative projects in food safety and microbial genomics.

Areas: Genomics • Food Microbiology • Data Science • Bioinformatics • Foodborne • Bacteria

Research

I work on the genomic characterization of bacterial isolates from food and environmental samples.
My interests include:

  • Antimicrobial resistance and virulence gene detection
  • Pangenome analysis and phylogeny
  • Developing open-source pipelines and reproducible scripts for genomics
